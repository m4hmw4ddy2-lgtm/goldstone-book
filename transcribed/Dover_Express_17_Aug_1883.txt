THE WALTHAMSTOW TRAGEDY.
Dover Express, 17 August 1883 (evening edition)

At Stratford Petty Sessions, before Mr Powell, the chairman, William Gouldstone, charged with the murder of his five children, was again brought up on remand.

[Article reports the renewed magisterial proceedings, 14-16 August 1883. Substantially overlaps in content with the IPN coverage of 18 August already transcribed (sources/images/Media-coverage/IPN Aug 18 1883 inside detail 1.png and detail 2.png), which covers the same hearing in greater legible detail: William Gouldstone's background as a sober, industrious whitesmith of five years' standing at the Falkirk Ironworks; description of him as "a man ordinarily by no means of the care-worn, roving-eyed description"; the close marriage and the eleven months' lodging with the Clarkes; his reserved disposition; the Sunday walks with Clarke; the cord/string evidence and the hammer; the full examination of Ada Hamilton's testimony as already established; and the magistrates' decision to commit Gouldstone for trial, with the case to be transferred to the Treasury for prosecution.]
