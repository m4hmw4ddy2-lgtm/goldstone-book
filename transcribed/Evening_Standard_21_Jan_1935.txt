Death After 52 Years in Broadmoor
Evening Standard, 21 January 1935

William Goldstone, a Walthamstow blacksmith, who 52 years ago was found guilty of the murder of his five children and found to be insane, has died in Broadmoor Criminal Lunatic Asylum. He was 80 years of age, and had been described as an ideal patient. He worked in the various wards.

TRANSCRIPTION NOTES
Source: Evening Standard, 21 January 1935
Repository image: sources/images/Media-coverage/Evening_Standard_21_Jan_1935_Death_of_William_G.jpg
The death notice. NOTE: gives his age as 80 -- this is WRONG by the established birth year (c.1857, making him approximately 77-78 in January 1935). All other established sources and the master notes timeline give his age at death as 78. The "52 years ago" figure is consistent with the murders (August 1883) to death (January 1935) interval, which is in fact closer to 51.5 years -- "52 years" is a reasonable rounding.
NOTE: His surname is rendered "Goldstone" (without the "u") in this 1935 notice, consistent with the family's later adoption of the "Goldstone" spelling (as used by Albert and descendants) rather than the original "Gouldstone" spelling used throughout the 1883 press and trial documents. This is a small but telling detail: even William's own death notice, 52 years later, uses the family's later, anglicised spelling rather than the name under which he was tried and convicted.
KEY: "described as an ideal patient. He worked in the various wards." This is the only known characterisation of William's 52 years of life inside Broadmoor. Belongs in the closing material of Part Three / opening of Part Four, marking the transition from the crime to its long aftermath.
