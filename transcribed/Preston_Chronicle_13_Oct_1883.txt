THE CONVICT GOULDSTONE.
Preston Chronicle, 13 October 1883

The High Sheriff of Essex received on Wednesday morning a communication from the Home Office, stating that the convict William Gouldstone, convicted of the murder of his five children at Walthamstow, had been certified to be of unsound mind, and that he will be removed to Broadmoor Criminal Lunatic Asylum as soon as the necessary documents can be completed. It was added that it might be intimated to Gouldstone that the capital sentence would not be carried out. This intimation was accordingly made to the convict by the Under Sheriff, Mr. Charles Gepp, shortly after the receipt of the letter, and Gouldstone received it with deep gratitude.
