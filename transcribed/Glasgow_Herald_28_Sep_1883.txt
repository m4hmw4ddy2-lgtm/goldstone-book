[GLASGOW HERALD]
28 September 1883

It is understood that the Home Secretary has decided to commute the death sentence passed on Gouldstone, the Walthamstow murderer, to penal servitude for life.

TRANSCRIPTION NOTES
Source: Glasgow Herald, 28 September 1883

Brief single-paragraph wire report, one of many regional papers carrying the same short notice of the Home Secretary's decision in the days before the formal reprieve order of 3 October 1883. Consistent with the Liverpool Daily Post and other "Commutation of Gouldstone's Sentence" items of the same week, already transcribed.
