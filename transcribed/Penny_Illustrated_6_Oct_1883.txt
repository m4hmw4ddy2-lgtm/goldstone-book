TRANSCRIPTION NOTES
Source: The Penny Illustrated Paper, 6 October 1883, p.218
Repository image: sources/images/Media-coverage/The Penny Illustrated Oct 6 1883.jpeg
This brief item, headed "GOULDSTONE'S MANIA," appears in the "Law and Crime" column alongside reports of other contemporary cases (the committal of O'Donnell for the shooting of James Carey, the Harley-Street Quarrel, and the "Bloomsbury Mystery"). The item is genuinely short -- it is cut off mid-sentence by the bottom of the column/page, immediately followed by advertisements, and there is no continuation elsewhere on this page. This appears to be the full extent of the item as originally printed, not a transcription gap.
NOTE: IMPORTANT -- earlier in this research session, a separate BMJ piece (BMJ Medical News, 6 October 1883, already transcribed in repository at transcribed/BMJ_6_October_1883_Transcription.txt) was sourced via dictation and contains substantially more detail under similar subject matter, including the family insanity details (aunt, great-uncle) and the claim that William intended to drown himself on the day of the murders. THIS PENNY ILLUSTRATED ITEM IS A DIFFERENT, SHORTER PIECE, confirming only the bare fact of the impending Broadmoor transfer and naming Dr Orange. Do not conflate the two -- they are separate publications on the same date with overlapping but distinct content.
KEY: Confirms the headline framing "Gouldstone's Mania" was current contemporary newspaper language for the case by this date -- useful period-accurate phrase if needed for a chapter title or section heading in Part Three.
