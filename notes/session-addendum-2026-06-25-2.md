## New Confirmed Facts — 25 June 2026

### William Madams — death in asylum
**Source:** National Archives MH 94/37, Lunacy Patients Admission Registers
- Name: Wm Madams
- Admitted: 4 September 1901, Chartham Asylum, Kent (Kent County Asylum near Canterbury — NOT Chatham)
- Died: 23 November 1901, Kent

**Sequence clarified:**
- 31 March 1901: Census — William Madams, Elizabeth, Albert (15), Lillian (10), Eliza (9) at home in Whitstable
- 19 June 1901: Albert enlists as Albert Goldstone, born Germany
- 4 September 1901: William Madams admitted to Chartham Asylum
- 23 November 1901: William Madams dies at Chartham
- Albert had already left before Madams was admitted. Elizabeth was left alone in Whitstable with two young daughters.

**Narrative significance:** A second man in Elizabeth's life succumbed to mental breakdown and was institutionalised. William Gouldstone in Broadmoor 1883, William Madams in Chartham 1901. Whether Albert knew Madams was deteriorating before he enlisted is unknown.

### Mrs Bayly identified
Mary Bayly (1816-1899), author of *Ragged Homes and How to Mend Them* (1860), social reformer working among London working poor, particularly Kensington Potteries/Notting Dale. A woman connected with Mrs Bayly's Refuge Home visited William in custody after committal and he showed repentance (Times 20 August 1883). Book now in repo: transcribed/Bayly_book.txt. Relevant as context for Part One (world William and Elizabeth came from) and Part Two (charitable infrastructure around working poor).

### Savage Private Nursing PDF
Read in full. Address to nursing staff of The Retreat, York, 23 November 1911. Key passage: Savage on patients who hear hallucinations — "the patient heard unkind words and thought they came from nurse, they were, in fact, only hallucinations of hearing." Shows Savage's deep clinical understanding of auditory hallucinations — exactly what William described (hearing voice of God). The gap between this humane, experienced clinician and his constrained courtroom performance in 1883 is the tragedy of Part Three.
