## SECTION 1: GENUINE UNRESOLVED CONTRADICTIONS

### 1.1 Address of the murder scene
Two confirmed addresses from separate documents:
- "2 Helen Cottages, Queens Road, West Walthamstow" — coroner's report 1935
- "8 Courtenay Place, St James's Street, Walthamstow" — Ada Hamilton's witness statement and all press

Most likely explanation: Helen Cottages was the terrace name; 8 Courtenay Place the street address. The 1935 coroner's report, compiled 52 years later, may have used an informal or outdated form.

**Action:** NLS historic maps (maps.nls.uk) OS 25-inch 1888-1913. Waltham Forest Local Studies Library rate books and street renumbering schedules. Resolve before Part One is drafted.

---

### 1.2 Penny Illustrated — mother's death
Penny Illustrated 6 October 1883 states William's mother was "found dead in a house in Bloomsbury-street, when her husband was discovered 'very drunk' in the adjoining kitchen." Emily Willett Gouldstone appears alive in the 1901 and 1911 census in Great Sampford.

**Most likely explanation:** Either a misreport, a confusion with another case, or a non-fatal incident. Emily was definitely alive in 1901.

**Action:** Check for any Bloomsbury incident involving a Gouldstone 1883-1884. Do not use in book without resolution.

## SECTION 4: CHATGPT ERRORS — DO NOT USE

- **Marsh Street** as murder address — hallucination. Address is 8 Courtenay Place, St James's Street, Walthamstow.
- **Kleptomania** — speculation from degraded OCR. Not in any source document.
- **Twins were girls** — error. All verified sources confirm twin boys.
- **"Chatham Asylum"** — institution was Chartham Asylum near Canterbury.
- **"Platen machine minder"** — incorrect. William was a whitesmith.
- **Fabricated transcript passages** — ChatGPT invented entire pages of witness testimony when challenged. All transcript text must be verified against repo source documents.
