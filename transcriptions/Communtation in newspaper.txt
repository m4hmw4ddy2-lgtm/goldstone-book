TRANSCRIPTION

COMMUTATION OF GOULDSTONE'S
SENTENCE.

The Home Secretary has requested two physicians to examine the convict Gouldstone, and report as to his mental condition.  The British Medical Journal says that the Home Secretary intends to commute the sentence on Gouldstone to penal servitude for life, "the evidence of insanity being sufficient to justify a modification of the sentence."  We venture to say that such a termination of the case would be absurd.  If there is sufficient evidence of insanity to justify a commutation of the sentence, it would follow as a matter of course that Gouldstone would be sent, not into penal servitude, but to a criminal lunatic asylum, where he would be properly cared for.  He is either a murderer of the worst type or an irresponsible lunatic.
