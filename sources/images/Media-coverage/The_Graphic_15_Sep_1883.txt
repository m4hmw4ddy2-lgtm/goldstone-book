THE GRAPHIC
15 September 1883, p.275 (extract)

LORD COLERIDGE continues to have a "good time," in American phrase. At Portsmouth a revenue cutter was placed at his disposal to carry him to Boston, where he was cordially received by the representatives of the Massachusetts Bar, and by General Butler, the Governor of the State. On Saturday he was a very neat speech in honour of the famous lawyers America has produced, and the ties of mutual regard and goodwill which should bind the two countries together. The Rochester Bar entertain him this week so far.

SIR W. HARCOURT has ordered the release next month of Dr Story, sentenced at the Bedfordshire Winter Assizes in 1881 to five years' penal servitude for incendiarism. The Doctor, who has always denied the charge, was always responsible for his actions, though all the time he has been three sun-stricken abroad.
