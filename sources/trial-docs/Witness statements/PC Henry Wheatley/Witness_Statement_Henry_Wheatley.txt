Henry Wheatley, Police Constable 208 N
Stationed at Leabridge Road

About 1/4 to 7 in the evening of the 8th August I was in charge of the prisoner, While he was in the cell I was in the passage. Prisoner said to me:
“I have had one extra draft of drink today to accomplish the job.”
“There is five of them gone to Glory, and It is a good job too”
About half past 7 o'clock the same night he said to me
“I am sorry for my wife and parents, but the children will be better off in heaven, if there is such a place; it is better than leaving them to the mercy of the world”
About 9 o’clock he said:
“I wish I had killed the little ones out of the way I don't know whether I hit them once or twice I have had this playing on my mind for a long time.”

Cross-examined: Prisoner did not mumble and talk to himself. I observed no traces of drink about the prisoner.
He first said he had killed the five, and afterwards that he wished he had killed the other two.
He appeared perfectly cool — not showy in the least.

Sworn this 18th August 1883
Henry Wheatley
[Signed] Nath. Powell
