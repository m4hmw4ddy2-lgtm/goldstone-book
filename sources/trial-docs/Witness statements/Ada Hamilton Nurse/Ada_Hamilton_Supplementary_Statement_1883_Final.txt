🧾 SUPPLEMENTARY STATEMENT – ADA HAMILTON
Pages 010–012 of 12
Resworn, 18 August 1883

I have known prisoner since the 7th June last.

I have had ample opportunity of observing prisoner’s demeanour.

Up to the time of this occurrence he appeared kind to his wife and children.

The twins were born on the 1st August.

After that I observed a marked difference in the prisoner.

He was never addicted to drink.

I have not seen him the worse for liquor but once.

After 1st August he appeared to be in a despondent state.

I have heard him complain of being short of money.

Never heard him talk of not being able to support the children.

Never heard him complain of his wife, or of showing his temper.

Never heard him say a cross word to his wife.

I never heard the prisoner say “She wished the children were dead.”

He never spoke to me on the Sunday previous, that he had a fit of kleptomania coming on.

I saw him sitting on the fire guard with his back to the fire, with a piece of cord in his hand.

I didn’t notice a slip noose in the string.

I saw the three children all laid on the floor.

At the time the murder was committed he seemed excited.

Signed: Ada Hamilton
Resworn this 18th August 1883
Signed: Nath. Powell
