🧾 WITNESS STATEMENT – GEORGE FOLKARD
Sworn 18 August 1883, Pages 1–2

---

PAGE 1:

George Folkard, sworn

I am an Inspector of the N Division stationed at Leabridge.

I was at the Police Station on the 8th instant when the prisoner was brought in by PC William Cheeseman.

Cheeseman said,
“This is a charge of murder, Sir. This man has murdered his five children.”

Prisoner said,
“That is right, Sir. I did it — now I am happy. I did it like a man to—”

I went to the house with Dr. Gould and returned to the station.

About 8 o’clock I then charged him with murdering his children Charles, Albert and Frederick, and attempting to murder his two male infants.

He replied,
“Quite right, Sir.”

“What condition was the missus in I suppose some of you gentlemen have been round, and—”

---

PAGE 2:

Dr. Gould replied to him, “Tolerable.”

He said to me, “Are the two young ones dead?”  
I said, “No.”

About half past 8 the following morning I charged him with the murder of the whole five children.

He replied:
“Yes, Sir. The other two are dead then.”

I said, “Yes.”

When I went to the house at 8 Courtenay Place I examined the cistern.  
The top of it is about six feet from the floor and about ten inches from the ceiling.  
The depth of water in the cistern was fourteen inches.

Cross-examined:  
He volunteered all this statement to me.  
He appeared very slightly excited.  
He was not vacant but was rather cool.

Sworn this 18th August 1883  
(signed) Nath. Powell  
(signed) George Folkard, Insp. N
