James Honey, Police Constable 41 N
Stationed at Leabridge Road

On the afternoon of Wednesday the 8th of August about 10 minutes to 6 I was on duty in High Street Walthamstow. From information received I went for Dr. Lorning and then to Number 8 Courtenay Road. When I got there Dr. Lorning was there. He was examining the bodies of three children who were in the kitchen. I saw Dr. Lorning take the piece of string produced from the neck of the boy Charles. Dr. Lorning handed it to me. I could see that the three children were dead. I then saw the other two children — one was bleeding from the mouth and both their heads appeared to be severely injured. I remained there until Dr. Gould came.

Cross-examined:
I saw the noose on the string which was loose round the child’s neck.

Sworn this 18th August 1883  
James Honey  
Nath. Powell
